Since October 2023, we have witnessed a genocide as it has been live-streamed around the world. Internationally, Israel has been condemned of mass starvation, and the targeted bombing of Palestinian hospitals, schools, and homes. The world has watched in horror through our phones as innocent civilians and clearly marked journalists have been murdered in plain sight.

The world has woken up.

Globally we have seen millions take to the streets calling for liberation for the Palestinian people. Locally we have seen students build encampments to protest universities ties with Zionism. We have seen port workers refuse to load weapon shipments. People from around the Bay Area, and around the world now see clearly: this is imperialism. This is apartheid, and we will not look away.

This shift is exactly what it takes to build a movement that can challenge even the mightiest of empires.

Why Culture Matters — Art as Resistance

An important lesson from this moment – and from every liberation struggle in history – is that cultural resistance is intrinsic to the struggle itself.

Culture is not separate from politics. It is where politics lives and breathes. It shapes how we see the world, what we believe is possible, and who we believe deserves dignity. For too long, the dominant culture has dehumanized Palestinians, erased their history, and justified their oppression.

As artists, we have a responsibility to challenge that narrative. To amplify voices that have been silenced. To create space for truth.

About the Project

We, the artists represented on this record, stand in firm solidarity with those resisting occupation and fighting for their right to self-determination.

Caroline Chung (Citizens Jazz) and Mike Walti (Wyldwood Studios) co-produced this record throughout 2025.
